## Path 1: PNG vision encoder — DEAD

Background-agent deep-dive. The PNGs are 4-panel matplotlib renders of
data already in the CSVs (formation tops, GR(MD), GR-vs-TVT). They are
*generated by the dataset publisher*, not hand-drawn by operators.

Two independent kill criteria:
  1. **No PNGs in hidden test** — only the local 3-well sample has them.
     Nothing to score against at inference.
  2. **No marginal information** — the formation tops drawn in the PNGs
     are the same `ANCC, ASTNU, ASTNL, EGFDU, EGFDL, BUDA` columns
     already on every train row.

The middle TVT panel of the train PNGs literally draws the answer for
the post-PS region (GR vs hidden TVT). Trivially extractable. Useless
for test.

Recommendation from the agent: do not pursue. We agree.

## Path 2: Pad-sister retrieval — DEAD as primary

`rogii/src/pad_sister.py`, `rogii/bench/pad_sister_score.py`.

Hypothesis: 53.6% of train wells have a centroid within 500 ft of
another (typical Eagle Ford pad spacing). 82.5% within 1000 ft. Sister
laterals share geology and operator practice; their TVT trajectories
should be a stronger prior than konbu17's row-level KNN.

Implementation: BallTree over centroids, score sisters by
`{centroid_dist, lateral_azimuth_alignment, Z_overlap, prefix_GR_corr}`,
average top-K sisters' TVT(MD) re-anchored at the query's prefix-median
TVT.

Result on 30-well holdout, K=5:

```
RMSE          34.93
bias         -15.82  (systematic underestimate)
max well     88.77
median well  23.25
```

Diagnosis is geological, not algorithmic. The worst wells have
**coverage = 1.00 and sister-score 0.7-0.86** (decent matches!) yet
bias -50 to -85 ft. Operators on the same pad steer their laterals
*independently* in the eval section. konbu17's row KNN incidentally
softens this by averaging across non-sister wells; whole-trajectory
averaging amplifies it.

Reusable: `PadSisterIndex.find_sisters` cheaply yields `sister_mean_tvt`,
`sister_tvt_spread`, `sister_gr_correlation`, `sister_centroid_distance`
features for any model that wants them. We did NOT wire them into v8;
the question is whether they add OOF beyond what's already there.

## Path 3: Particle-filter geosteering — DEAD as primary

`rogii/src/particle_geosteer.py`, `rogii/bench/particle_geosteer_score.py`.

Hypothesis: the competition is framed as geosteering. TVT is a hidden
Markov state evolving along MD. Horizontal GR vs typewell GR at
predicted TVT is the natural observation. konbu17's beam-search Viterbi
is a discrete approximation; a continuous PF should do better and can
absorb v8's spatial prediction as a soft prior.

Implementation: 4000 particles, transition = small step + rare jumps,
likelihood = Gaussian on `gr_horizontal − typewell_GR(predicted_TVT)`,
optional Gaussian prior at `tvt_formula_row`. Systematic resampling on
ESS threshold.

Result on 20-well holdout:

```
formula only (no GBM)       rmse=17.58  bias=+4.82  max well=40.57
PF, no prior                rmse=56.70  bias=-10.66 max well=101.05
PF + spatial prior          rmse=18.79  bias=+3.95  max well=44.64
blend(formula, PF) alpha=0.5 rmse=17.88 bias=+4.39  max well=42.35
```

The PF without a prior **drifts catastrophically** — the GR-typewell
likelihood alone is too noisy. With the formula prior, the PF's
stochastic resampling slightly *worsens* the formula. The GBM in v8
(which pulls the formula RMSE 17 → 12) is doing the actual heavy
lifting here.

Reusable: PF posterior std could be a useful uncertainty feature for the
GBM, but v8 already has neighbour-distance and KNN-std features that
serve the same role.

## Path 4: Neural ANCC field — LIVE (full results)

`rogii/src/neural_ancc.py`, `rogii/bench/neural_ancc_score.py`,
`rogii/bench/neural_ancc_results.json`.

Hypothesis: konbu17's row-level KNN and per-well plane fit are LOCAL
estimators. They fail wherever neighbours are sparse, producing
catastrophic well-RMSE outliers. A small MLP with sinusoidal positional
encoding (NeRF-style) on (X, Y) learns a *global* smooth-surface
representation.

**Full 5-fold GroupKFold OOF over 765 wells / 5,040,554 rows:**

```
variant              ANCC pool   TVT med   TVT p90   TVT max   wells>60ft
KNN (K=20, IDW)         30.74     12.30     42.58    300.85       46
MLP no_PE               45.53     22.06     49.16     99.54       45
MLP + PE L=8            23.52     15.19     33.25    142.79       13
MLP + PE L=16           25.65     17.73     35.89    115.00       14
MLP + PE L=8 multi      24.10     14.67     32.19    165.66       11
MLP + PE L=8 ens(3)     23.27     14.32     31.89    147.61       11
```

Per-fold TVT max well RMSE (the load-bearing stat):

```
fold   KNN     MLP+PE-L8   MLP+PE-L8 multi
 0     87.8     97.7         98.6
 1    110.4     51.5         49.9
 2    300.9     68.9         57.7  <-- collapses by 80%
 3    132.4    107.4        106.8
 4    162.8    142.8        165.7
```

**Findings:**

1. **PE matters.** No-PE MLP is uniformly worse than KNN (45.5 vs 30.7
   pooled ANCC). With L=8 PE, MLP beats KNN on ANCC by 23%.
2. **MLP fixes the catastrophic tail.** On KNN's top-10 worst wells
   (TVT RMSE 114–301 ft), MLP wins on every one of them, with
   improvements of −20 to **−250 ft**. The 301-ft fold-2 outlier
   collapses to 58 ft.
3. **MLP loses on the already-easy wells.** On 454/765 wells KNN has
   lower TVT RMSE (median 14.3 ft for KNN-easy wells; MLP is ~3–4 ft
   worse). KNN's local interpolation of dense neighbours is genuinely
   sharper than a 256-hidden MLP for the typical well. This is the
   median gap (12.30 vs 14.67).
4. **Catastrophic-well count drops 4×.** Wells with TVT RMSE > 60 ft go
   from 46 (KNN) to 11 (MLP multi). Wells > 30 ft from 133 to 99.
5. **L=8 ≥ L=16.** L=16 gives marginally worse pooled ANCC and TVT;
   extra high-freq channels add overfitting risk.
6. **Multi-output (all 6 formations) is best on outlier-control,**
   marginally beating ANCC-only on TVT p90/max/wells>60ft because the
   shared trunk regularizes.
7. **3-seed ensemble: marginal.** Pooled ANCC 23.27 vs 23.52; TVT max
   *increases*. Not worth 3× compute.
8. **Wall-time on M1 Pro MPS:** ~10–12 s fit + <1 s inference per fold.
   End-to-end 5-fold OOF: ~3 min. On Kaggle T4 GPU: faster.

**The right play: STACK, not replace.** KNN dominates on ~60% of wells
(dense-neighbour case); MLP dominates on the catastrophic-outlier tail.
Two practical paths:

  - **Cheap:** add `mlp_ancc_pred` and `mlp_tvt_formula = -Z + mlp_ancc
    + b_prefix_median` as features in v8's LGB stack. The GBM learns
    the gate from `knn_row_dist` itself.
  - **Cleaner:** distance-aware blend at inference,
      `tvt = w_knn · tvt_knn + (1 − w_knn) · tvt_mlp`,
      `w_knn = exp(−knn_row_dist / τ)`,
    calibrate τ on OOF.

The cheap path is what v9 should ship. v8's GBM already exposes
`knn_row_dist`; adding two MLP-derived features is mechanical.

## What this leaves

- **v8 ships first.** OOF 12.03 (matches konbu17 12.11) is already a
  big win over v6 (LB 13.854). Push when the 8-h guard clears.
- **v9 candidate**: neural-ANCC replacing the row KNN inside the v8
  feature builder. Estimated 1 day of work + validation.
- **Anisotropic kriging** (rogii/src/aniso_kriging.py, drafted earlier)
  is now lower priority than neural-ANCC since the MLP outperforms KNN
  by a large margin and likely subsumes anisotropy.
- **Pad-sister features** worth a quick A/B test inside v8's GBM
  (sister_mean_tvt, sister_tvt_spread). Low effort.
- **PF features**: skip. v8 has equivalent uncertainty signals.

## Process note

All four paths were genuinely different from "tune v8 better". Three
failed, one survived — that's a healthy hit rate for boldness. The
failures came back with concrete reasons (test PNGs absent, eval-time
trajectory divergence, GR likelihood too noisy) rather than mush.
